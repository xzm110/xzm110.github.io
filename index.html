每日一题 day09/10

给定一个锥 $K$, 定义 $K$ 的对偶锥 $K^\star$ 为
$$
K^\star = { y | <x, y> \ge 0, \forall x \in K }
$$
如果 $K$ 的对偶锥 $K^\star = K$, 称 $K$ 是自对偶锥。
证明：半正定锥 $S^n_+ = { A \in R^{n\times n} | x^T A x \ge 0, \forall x \in R^n }$ 是自对偶锥。

check $(S^n_+)^\star \subset S^n_+$: 取一个 $Y \in (S^n_+)^\star$, 假设存在 $x \in R^n$, 使得 $x^T Y x \le0$, 那么 $<xx^T, Y> = tr((xx^T)^T Y) = x^TYx <0$ 矛盾；
check $S^n_+ \subset (S^n_+)^\star$: 任意 $A, B \in S^n_+$, 把 $A$ 合同变换分解， $A = \sum_{i=1}^n \lambda_i t_i t_i^T$, 那么 $<A, B> = tr(A^TB) = tr(AB) = \sum tr(\lambda_i t_i t_i^T B) = \sum \lambda_i t_i^T B t_i \ge 0$
as desired.

For Markov chain, assume $f_{ii} < 1, f_{jj} < 1$,
proof $\sum_{n=1}^\infty P_{ij}^n < \infty$
and $f_{ij} =\frac{\sum_{n=1}^\infty P_{ij}^n}{1 + \sum_{n=1}^\infty P_{jj}^n}$

Note that
$$
\begin{align}
\sum_{n=1}^\infty {P_{ij}^n} &= \sum_{n=1}^\infty {\sum_{l=1}^n{f_{ij}^l {P_{jj}^{n-l}}}} \\
&= \sum_{l=1}^\infty { \sum_{n=l}^\infty {f_{ij}^l P_{jj}^{n-l} } } \\
&= \sum_{l=1}^\infty {\sum_{n=0}^\infty {f_{ij}^l {P_{jj}^{n}} } } \\
&= \sum_{l=1}^\infty {f_{ij}^l \sum_{n=0}^\infty { P_{jj}^{n}} } \\
&= f_{ij} \left( \sum_{n=0}^\infty P_{jj}^n \right) \\
&= f_{ij} \left( \frac1{1 - f_{jj}} \right) \\
&\le \frac1{1 - f_{jj} } \\
&< \infty \\
\end{align}
$$

By the proof above, specifically the last equation
$f_{ij} = \frac{\sum_{n=1}^\infty P_{ij}^n}{1 + \sum_{n=1}^\infty P_{jj}^n}$

每日一题 day07/10

proof Gnedenko law of large number: assume $X_i$ is a sequence of r.v. with finite expectation,
denote $Y_n = \frac1n\sum_{i=1}^n X_i, a_n = \sum_{i=1}^n EX_i$,
then $X_i$ obey weak law of large number is equivalent to $E\frac{(Y_n - a_n)^2}{1 + (Y_n - a_n)^2} \to 0$.

$\Rightarrow$:
$$
\begin{align}
E\frac{(Y_n - a_n)^2}{1 + (Y_n - a_n)^2} &= \int \frac{(y-a_n)^2}{1+(y-a_n)^2} dF_{n}(y) \\
&= \int_0^\epsilon + \int_\epsilon^\infty \\
&\le \epsilon + \int_\epsilon^\infty dF_m(y) \\
&= \epsilon + P(|Y_n - a_n| \ge \epsilon) \\
&\to 0
\end{align}
$$

$\Leftarrow$: similar to Chebyshev law of large number,
$$
\begin{align}
P(|Y_n - a_n | \ge \epsilon) &= \int_{|y-a_n| \ge \epsilon} dF_n(y) \\
& \frac{1 + \epsilon^2}{\epsilon^2} \int_{|y-a_n| \ge \epsilon} \frac{(y-a_n)^2}{1 + (y-a_n)^2} dF_n(y) \\
&\le \frac{1 + \epsilon^2}{\epsilon^2} \int_{R} \frac{(y-a_n)^2}{1 + (y-a_n)^2} dF_n(y) \\
&= \frac{1 + \epsilon^2}{\epsilon^2} E\frac{(Y_n - a_n)^2}{1 + (Y_n - a_n)^2} \\
&\to 0
\end{align}
$$
as desired.

每日一题 day06/10

今天先证明一个定理，再用来做一个小题目。
或许我应该复习一点概率理论了，我常常因为自己菜而感到难过，也因为自己理论基础不扎实而难过。

假设有一列独立同分布的随机变量 $X_1, X_2, X_3…$, 同时 $N(t)$ 是一个强度为 $\lambda$ 的与 $X_i$独立的 Poisson process, 定义 $X(t) = \sum_{i=1}^{N(t)} X_i$ 是一个 compound Poisson process.

Assume $EX_i^2 < \infty$, proof $EX(t) = \lambda t EX_1, VarX(t) = \lambda t EX_1^2$.

Proof it by take condition on $N(t)$,
$$
\begin{align}
EX(t) &= E\left[ EX(t) | N(t) \right] \\
&= E\left[ N(t) EX_1 \right] \\
&= \lambda t EX_1 \\
\end{align}
$$
To calculate $VarX(t)$, we use the decomposition of $VarX(t)$ by taking condition on $N(t)$.
$$
\begin{align}
VarX(t) &= Var\left[ E\left[ X(t) | N(t) \right] \right] + E Var\left[ X(t)| N(t) \right] \\
&= \lambda t (EX_1)^2 + E N(t) VarX_1 \\
&= \lambda t EX_1^2
\end{align}
$$
as desired.

Another proof is using the moment generating function.
$$
\begin{align}
Ee^{sX(t)} &= E\left[Ee^{s\sum_{i=1}^{N(t)} X_i} | N(t)\right] \\
&= E\left[ \left(Ee^{sX_1}\right)^{N(t)} \right] \\
&= e^{ \lambda t\left(Ee^{sX_1}-1\right)} \\
\end{align}
$$
Take derivates of the moment generating function $Ee^{sX(t)}$ with respect to $s$ and set $s=0$, then the result is derived.

A small example:
Assume $X_i$ is multinomial distributed with probability $P(X_i=1) = \frac18, P(X_i = 2) = \frac18, P(X_i=3) = \frac12, P(X_i=4) = \frac14$, and rate $\lambda = 3$
Calculate $EX(10)$
Pretty easy $EX_1 = \frac{23}8$. Hence, $EX(10) = 10 \times 3 \times \frac{23}8 = \frac{345}4$.

每日一题 day05/10

I decide to pay more attention to the generalization of Poisson distribution, that is nonhomogeneous Poisson process, compound Poisson random variables and process, and conditional Poisson process.

Let ${X(t), t \ge 0}$ be a compound Poisson process with $X(t) = \sum_{i=1}^{N(t)} X_i$, and suppose that the $X_i$ can only assume a finite set of possible values, Argue that, for $t$ large, the distribution of $X(t)$ is approximately normal.

The proof is pretty simple(?) by large number theory. Note that $N(t) \to \infty$ as $t \to infty$, and $X_i$ is i.i.d.with finite mean and variance, by large number theory,
$$
\frac{X(t) - EX(t)}{VarX(t)} \to N(0,1)
$$

每日一题 day04/10

Assume ${ N(t) }$ is a Poisson process with rate $\lambda$, evaluate $EN(t) N(t+s), 0 < t, s$.

Solve:
$$
\begin{align}
EN(t)N(t+s) &= EN(t)\left(N(t+s) - N(t) \right) + EN(t)^2 \\
&= EN(t) E\left(N(t+s) - N(t) \right) + EN(t)^2 \\
&= EN(t) EN(s) + EN(t)^2 \\
&= \lambda t \lambda s + Var(N(t)) + \left(EN(t)\right)^2 \\
&= \lambda^2 ts + \lambda t + \lambda^2 t^2 \\
&= \lambda^2 t(t+s) + \lambda t
\end{align}
$$
The second equation is a result of independent increments of Poisson porcess.

definition of some sets in optimization

  1. convex set
    Set S is a convex set iff $\forall x_1, x_2 \in S, \theta x_1 + (1-\theta)x_2 \in S, \forall \theta \in (0,1)$.
  2. affine set
    Set S is an affine set iff $\forall x_1, x_2 \in S, \theta x_1 + (1-\theta)x_2 \in S, \forall \theta \in \mathbb{R}$.
  3. cone
    Set S in a cone iff $\forall x \in S, \forall \theta \ge 0, \theta x \in S$.

Remark:
$S \subset \mathbb{R}^n$.
