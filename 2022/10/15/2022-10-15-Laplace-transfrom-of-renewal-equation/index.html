Proof Laplace transform of renewal equation

Apply Laplace transform of the renewal equation,
$$
\begin{aligned}
\int_0^\infty e^{-st}M(t) dt &= \int_0^\infty e^{-st}F(t) dt + \int_0^\infty \int_0^t e^{-st} M(t-y) d F(y) dt\
\end{aligned}
$$

Denote left hand side as $\mathcal{L}[M(t)]$,
$$
\begin{align}
\int_{0}^\infty \int_0^t e^{-st} M(t-y) dF(y) dt &= \int_0^\infty e^{-st} dt\int_{0}^t M(t-y) f(y) dy \\
&= \int_0^\infty f(y)dy \int_{y}^\infty e^{-st} M(t-y) dt \\
&= \int_0^\infty e^{-sy}f(y)dy \mathcal{L}[M(t)] \\
&= \mathcal{L}[f(t)] \mathcal{L}[M(t)] \\
\end{align}
$$
Hence,
$$
\mathcal{L}[M(t)] = \frac{\mathcal{L}[F(t)]}{1 - \mathcal{L}[f(t)] }
$$
Then alpply inverse Lalapce transform, $M(t)$ is derived.
