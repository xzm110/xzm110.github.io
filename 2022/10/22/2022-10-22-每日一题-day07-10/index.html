proof Gnedenko law of large number: assume XiX_iXi​ is a sequence of r.v. with finite expectation,
denote Yn=1n∑i=1nXi,an=∑i=1nEXiY _n = \frac1n \sum _{i=1}^n X_i, a _n = \sum _{i=1}^n EX_iYn​=n1​∑i=1n​Xi​,an​=∑i=1n​EXi​,
then XiX_iXi​ obey weak law of large number is equivalent to E(Yn−an)21+(Yn−an)2→0E\frac{(Y _n - a _n)^2}{1 + (Y _n - a _n)^2} \to 0E1+(Yn​−an​)2(Yn​−an​)2​→0.

⇒\Rightarrow⇒:

\begin{align} E\frac{(Y_n - a_n)^2}{1 + (Y_n - a_n)^2} &= \int \frac{(y-a_n)^2}{1+(y-a_n)^2} dF_{n}(y) \\\\ &= \int_0^\epsilon + \int_\epsilon^\infty \\\\ &\le \epsilon + \int_\epsilon^\infty dF_m(y) \\\\ &= \epsilon + P(|Y_n - a_n| \ge \epsilon) \\\\ &\to 0 \end{align}

⇐\Leftarrow⇐: similar to Chebyshev law of large number,

\begin{align} P(|Y_n - a_n | \ge \epsilon) &= \int_{|y-a_n| \ge \epsilon} dF_n(y) \\\\ & \frac{1 + \epsilon^2}{\epsilon^2} \int_{|y-a_n| \ge \epsilon} \frac{(y-a_n)^2}{1 + (y-a_n)^2} dF_n(y) \\\\ &\le \frac{1 + \epsilon^2}{\epsilon^2} \int_{R} \frac{(y-a_n)^2}{1 + (y-a_n)^2} dF_n(y) \\\\ &= \frac{1 + \epsilon^2}{\epsilon^2} E\frac{(Y_n - a_n)^2}{1 + (Y_n - a_n)^2} \\\\ &\to 0 \end{align}

as desired.
