For Markov chain, assume $f_{ii} < 1, f_{jj} < 1$,
proof $\sum_{n=1}^\infty P_{ij}^n < \infty$
and $f_{ij} =\frac{\sum_{n=1}^\infty P_{ij}^n}{1 + \sum_{n=1}^\infty P_{jj}^n}$

Note that
$$
\begin{align}
\sum_{n=1}^\infty {P_{ij}^n} &= \sum_{n=1}^\infty {\sum_{l=1}^n{f_{ij}^l {P_{jj}^{n-l}}}} \\
&= \sum_{l=1}^\infty { \sum_{n=l}^\infty {f_{ij}^l P_{jj}^{n-l} } } \\
&= \sum_{l=1}^\infty {\sum_{n=0}^\infty {f_{ij}^l {P_{jj}^{n}} } } \\
&= \sum_{l=1}^\infty {f_{ij}^l \sum_{n=0}^\infty { P_{jj}^{n}} } \\
&= f_{ij} \left( \sum_{n=0}^\infty P_{jj}^n \right) \\
&= f_{ij} \left( \frac1{1 - f_{jj}} \right) \\
&\le \frac1{1 - f_{jj} } \\
&< \infty \\
\end{align}
$$

By the proof above, specifically the last equation
$f_{ij} = \frac{\sum_{n=1}^\infty P_{ij}^n}{1 + \sum_{n=1}^\infty P_{jj}^n}$
