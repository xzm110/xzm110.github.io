2022-12-20-derivative-of-log-det

Proove that ∂log⁡∣X∣∂X=X−1T\frac{\partial \log |X|}{\partial X} = {X^{-1}}^T∂X∂log∣X∣​=X−1T.

Proof: by definition,

\begin{align} \frac{\log|X+tV| - \log|X|}{t} &= \frac{\log|I + tX^{-1/2}VX^{-1/2}|}{t} \\ \end{align}

note that X−1/2VX−1/2X^{-1/2}VX^{-1/2}X−1/2VX−1/2 is symmetric and donate the eigen value by λi,i=1,2,...,n\lambda_i, i=1, 2, ..., nλi​,i=1,2,...,n.

\begin{align} \lim_{t\to0}\frac{\log|X+tV| - \log|X|}{t} &= \lim_{t\to0}\frac{\log|I + tX^{-1/2}VX^{-1/2}|}{t} \\ &= \lim_{t\to0}\frac{\sum \log(1 + t\lambda_i)}{t} \\ &= \sum \lambda_i \\ &= tr(X^{-1/2}VX^{-1/2}) \\ &= \langle X^{-T}, V \rangle \end{align}

Hence,
